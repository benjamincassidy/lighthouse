# Organizing Your Work

Lighthouse doesn't impose a folder structure — a project is just a root folder, with Groups as far down as you want to nest them, and one Extras group for everything else. Here's how to put that structure to good use.

## Start Simple

You don't need to plan a full structure up front. A single "Chapters" group with a handful of notes is a complete project. Add more groups as the shape of the work becomes clear — Lighthouse's group tree is just as easy to reorganize on day 30 as on day one.

## Group by the Unit You Think In

Pick groups that match how you actually think about the project, not an arbitrary convention:

- **Novels:** one group per act or part, or just one flat "Chapters" group with a note per chapter
- **Theses/dissertations:** one group per chapter (introduction, literature review, methodology, conclusion)
- **Blog series:** "Published" and "Drafts" groups
- **Documentation:** one group per section or product area

## Nest When It Helps, Not by Default

Groups can nest to any depth, but nesting is a tool, not an obligation. A single flat group of chapters is often easier to scan than three levels of "Book / Part / Chapter" folders. Nest when a group genuinely has natural subdivisions, not because the option exists.

## Let Extras Absorb Everything Else

Character notes, worldbuilding, research, outlines, meeting notes — all of it belongs in the **Extras** group. It behaves exactly like any other group (you can nest subgroups inside it too), with one difference: none of it counts toward your word goal. See [Groups & Extras](/core-concepts/groups/).

## Use Sheet Titles, Not Just Filenames

Since a sheet's title in the Library is the first line of the file, a chapter that opens with "Chapter One" or a descriptive first sentence is easier to scan in the Sheet List than a dozen files all titled "Untitled." The filename still matters for links — it's shown as a secondary detail — but the first line is what you'll actually see while browsing.

## Reorder Freely

Both groups and sheets support drag-and-drop reordering in the Library. Sheet order is what [Exporting](/features/exporting/) uses when compiling a project, so keep it in the sequence you'd want the final document to read in.

## Review Periodically

As a project evolves:
- Merge notes that ended up too fragmented (see [Splitting & Merging](/features/splitting-and-merging/))
- Split a note that's grown into two logical sections
- Move material that turned out to be research, not prose, into Extras
