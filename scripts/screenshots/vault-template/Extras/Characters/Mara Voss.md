Mara Voss

Age 34. Textbook editor in the city; grew up at Kestrel Point as the keeper's daughter. Left at 22 after her mother's death, hasn't been back since — until the opening chapter.

**Voice:** dry, guarded, given to counting things (the light's interval especially) as a self-soothing habit inherited from childhood. Softens slowly across the book, not all at once.

**Arc:** distant from her father → resentful of the light → understands the light was never the rival she thought it was → chooses to become a keeper herself.

**Physical:** her father's eyes (noted by Mrs. Pell in Ch. 2), otherwise takes after her mother. Not particularly strong or outdoorsy by trade — her competence in the storm chapters should read as earned in the moment, not as a hidden talent she had all along.

Open threads: what exactly happened with her mother (hinted, never fully stated — keep it that way through Act Two, pay it off in Act Three or leave for book two).
