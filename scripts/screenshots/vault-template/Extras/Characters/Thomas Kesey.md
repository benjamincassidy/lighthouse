Thomas Kesey

Mara's father. 71. Keeper of the Kestrel Point light for thirty years, second-generation — his own mother taught him the fourteen-second interval.

**Voice:** spare, understated, allergic to sentiment in speech even when he's clearly feeling it (see the log entries — he says more on paper than out loud). Dry humor surfaces rarely and always lands harder for it.

**Function in the story:** not an obstacle for Mara to overcome, but a person she has to see clearly for the first time. His stubbornness about the light is never really about the light — it's about believing the work matters even when no one's watching, which is the thing he's actually trying to teach her, badly, his whole life.

**Do not soften him into a simple "gruff but loving" type.** He should still be capable of the exact silences that pushed Mara away for twelve years. The reconciliation has to be earned by the storm sequence, not handed out early for sympathy.

Backstory notes: widowed roughly ten years ago (confirm timeline against Mara's age-22 departure). Trust board conflict has presumably been simmering for at least a year before Ch. 1 — reference an earlier board letter somewhere in Act One if it needs more setup.
