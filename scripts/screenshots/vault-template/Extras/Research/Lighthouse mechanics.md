Working notes on lighthouse operation, for consistency across chapters.

**Light interval:** established as fourteen seconds throughout — keep this exact number consistent every time it's mentioned (Ch. 1, 3, 6, 8). It's doing thematic work as Mara's counting motif, so precision matters more here than it would for a background detail.

**Fresnel lens:** rotates via a clockwork or motor-driven mechanism; historically some were rotated by a weight-and-pulley system the keeper had to wind, similar to a grandfather clock. Worth considering whether Kestrel Point's tower still uses a manual-wind mechanism — would explain why the light needs regular hands-on attention beyond just cleaning, and strengthens the automation-conflict subplot.

**Fog signal:** traditionally a diaphone or horn, sounded on a timed cycle or by hand in an emergency. Chapter 5's hand-operated signal should be plausible for an older station that hasn't been fully modernized — that's consistent with the trust board wanting to automate it.

**Keeper duties, roughly:** lamp servicing and lens cleaning (daily), fog signal maintenance, weather logging, radio watch, general structural upkeep. A two-keeper station was standard historically; Kestrel Point being single-keeper by this point in time underscores how stretched Thomas is, and why the board sees automation as cost-effective.

**Automation history (real-world grounding):** many real lighthouses were automated between the 1960s–1990s, with the last manually-keepered stations converting even later in some countries. A present-day setting where one holdout station still has a resident keeper is plausible without needing to be a period piece — don't accidentally imply this is a historical novel.
